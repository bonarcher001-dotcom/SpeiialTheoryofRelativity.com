The Inertial Frame of Reference

The Foundation of Special Relativity

What is an Inertial Frame?

In physics, a frame of reference is basically a coordinate system used to measure the position and motion of objects. An Inertial Frame is a frame that is either at rest or moving at a constant velocity.

Crucially, in an inertial frame, Newton’s First Law holds true: an object stays at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force. There is no acceleration involved!

Why It Matters to Einstein

Special Relativity is built on two main pillars (postulates) that only apply to inertial frames:

  • The Principle of Relativity: The laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames. Whether you are on a train moving smoothly at 100mph or standing on the platform, experiments will yield the same results.
  • The Constancy of Speed of Light: The speed of light in a vacuum (c) is the same for all observers, regardless of their motion relative to the light source.
